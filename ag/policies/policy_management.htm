Policy Management Functions

The following policy management functions apply to policy definitions in the Policies folder. They are available on the Policy Summary drop-down menu or via the Actions Portlet on the Policies > Details page.

back to top

Add Policy

Used to add policy definitions that are used to manage web service endpoints.

  • A policy is initialized with a default configuration which you can customize to address the unique requirements of your web service management system.
  • After a policy is configured, you then "attach" it to objects it will be managing including Organizations, Services, Operations, Bindings, and Access Points.

Select Add Policy on the main Policies Summary page, select a policy type, and assign a name and description. The Add Policy Wizard creates a policy instance that you can modify on the Policy Details page.

back to top

Attach Policy

Policies defined in the policies object (i.e., Policies Folder) can be assigned to an organization, and to six different elements of a service. Each Policy assignment is based on the business requirements defined for policies supported in each policy category (i.e., Compliance, Operational, QoS) in addition to custom defined policies.

  • When a policy is assigned at the "Service Details" level, the policy is "referenced" in all other elements of the service (i.e, operations, bindings, access points).
  • When a policy is referenced in a "Policy Attachments Portlet" it is preceded with "(from <object name>)."
  • Referenced policies can only be removed at the source attachment point (i.e., in the Policy Attachments Portlet of the "service").
  • Additional policies can be added to each service element using the "Policy Attachments Portlet" in each Management Console section.

Go to the Policy Attachments portlet on the Policy Details page where you would like to attach a policy and select Manage. Select a policy from the tree and save the configuration.

Change Organization

Allows you to move the current policy configuration to a new "Organization."

  • Note that referenced policies (i.e., those that are attached to an object) can only be moved within the current Policy Scope (i.e., Organization, Service, Operation, etc.).
  • If you wish to move a policy to a different "Organization" the references must stay in scope.
  • To determine what references a policy might have, select "View Policy References" for the current policy.
  • To remove policy references, unattach the policy via the Policy Attachments Portlet.

back to top

Copy Policy

Allows you to replicate a policy definition and assign a new "Policy Name" and "Policy Key." Using this function you can configure elements that represent core functionality of a policy, replicate them to a new policy, and then perform additional customization on the copy.

back to top

Delete Policy

Deletes the selected policy definition. In order to delete a policy, it cannot be "Referenced" (i.e., attached to one or more objects). The number of policy references are indicated in the "#" column on the Policies Summary page and can be viewed by selecting the "View Policy References."

If you would like to delete a policy, you must remove the policy attachment via Policy Attachments Portlet.

back to top

Export Policy

Provides a method of exporting a policy definition to a "Package" file. This "Package" file can then be imported into a different Policy Manager deployment using the "Import Package" function.

back to top

View Policy References

Displays a list of objects that the current policy is referencing.
